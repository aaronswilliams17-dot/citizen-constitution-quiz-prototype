# Citizen Constitution ‚Äì Quiz Prototype

A rapid product prototype for a nonpartisan civic education app designed to help users understand the U.S. Constitution through short, daily quizzes with source-based explanations.

This project was created as a time-boxed product exercise to demonstrate problem-solving, communication, and ethical AI design under tight timelines.

---

## üîç Problem

Many people want to better understand the U.S. Constitution and how it functions in practice, but existing resources are often:
- overly technical,
- politically framed,
- or difficult to engage with consistently.

This creates a barrier to informed civic participation.

---

## üí° Solution

A lightweight web-based app concept that:
- delivers short daily quizzes,
- explains answers in plain language,
- cites official, nonpartisan source material,
- and reinforces learning through repetition.

This prototype focuses on **clarity, integrity, and user trust** rather than feature completeness.

---

## ‚öñÔ∏è Design Constraints & Principles

- **Nonpartisan by design**  
  No political opinions or speculative interpretations.

- **Source-locked content**  
  Explanations are based only on official documents such as:
  - the U.S. Constitution
  - Supreme Court constitutional rulings
  - other verified government sources

- **Explainability first**  
  Every answer includes a clear explanation and a source reference.

- **Accuracy over automation**  
  Speed and scale are secondary to correctness and trust.

## ü§ñ About the ‚ÄúAI‚Äù in This Prototype

This prototype uses **simulated AI logic** rather than a live generative model.

The system:
- selects questions from a predefined source set,
- presents explanations tied directly to those sources,
- and mimics daily quiz behavior.

This approach was chosen intentionally to:
- avoid hallucinations,
- maintain transparency,
- and clearly demonstrate system rules and constraints.

A real AI model could be integrated later within these same boundaries.

## üö´ Explicit Non-Goals

The following were intentionally excluded from the prototype:
- user accounts or authentication
- personalization or data tracking
- real-time AI API integration
- mobile platform builds
- political analysis or opinion content

These decisions were made to prioritize correctness, clarity, and speed.
